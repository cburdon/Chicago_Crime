Data Breakdown




Volume of vehicles directly correlates with number of incidents.
Each datapoint on the graph represents number of incidents per hour on a particular day.
The Y axis represents the number of incidents on any given hour.
The X axis the total volume of cars within a particular hour as measured by traffic sensors/detectors recorded by the city of Austin.

Average speed directly correlates with number of incidents.
The lower the speed the higher the amount of incidents.
The Y axis represents the number of incidents on any given hour.
The X axis represents the average speed of a vehicle.

This pie chart shows the distribution for the type of reported issues in our dataset.
The top 3 issues are traffic hazards, urgent crashes, and crash services.
Here is the link to the definitions of each issue.

Traffic incidents directly correlates with rush hour ranges.
The highest peaks tend to be between 4pm to 6pm.
The second highest peaks tend to be between 7am to 9am.
